DisneyLand
  • Refers specifically to the first-ever Disney theme park created by Walt Disney, which opened in Anaheim, California, in 1955.
  • It also commonly refers to other parks with "Disneyland" in their name, such as Disneyland Paris or Hong Kong Disneyland.
Disney Park
  • A general term for any theme park owned, operated, or licensed by The Walt Disney Company.
  • Examples:
    • Disneyland Park in California (Anaheim).
    • Magic Kingdom in Florida (Orlando).
    • Tokyo Disneyland, Shanghai Disneyland, etc.
  • Parks are typically part of larger resorts and are themed around Disney characters, movies, and storytelling.
Disney Resort
  • Refers to an entire property that includes one or more Disney parks, hotels, shopping areas, and other entertainment facilities.
  • Examples:
    • Disneyland Resort (California): Includes Disneyland Park, Disney California Adventure, and Downtown Disney.
    • Walt Disney World Resort (Florida): Includes four parks, water parks, hotels, and Disney Springs.
    • Tokyo Disney Resort : Includes Tokyo Disneyland and Tokyo DisneySea.
  • Parks are typically part of larger resorts and are themed around Disney characters, movies, and storytelling.
